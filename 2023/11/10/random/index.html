Random Signals

Posted on 2023-11-10 Edited on 2023-11-11 In noise

For power signal , the PSD, \(S_x(f)\), gives the distribution of power in \(x(t)\) versus frequency. Since \(S_x(f)\) is a density, it has units of \(W/Hz\).

Formally, the power spectral density is defined as

\[ S_x(f) = \mathcal{F}\{R_x(\tau)\} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} R_x(\tau)e^{-j2\pi f\tau}d\tau \]

where \(R_x(\tau)\) is the autocorrelation function of the signal \(x(t)\) computed as per - deterministic signals: a time average - random signals: a statistical average

These two definitions are equivalent if \(x(t)\) is ergodic

\[\begin{align} R(\omega) &= \mathcal{F}\{R_x(t)\} = \mathcal{F}\{x(t) * x(-t)\} \\ &= \mathcal{F}\{x(t)\}\mathcal{F}\{x(-t)\} \\ & = X(\omega) X^*(\omega) = |X(\omega)|^2 \end{align}\]
